## 1. Game Overview

**Harvest Moon: More Friends of Mineral Town** is the female-protagonist companion to
*Friends of Mineral Town* (GBA, 2003–2004), developed by Natsume. It is a remake/companion
to the PlayStation 1 title *Back to Nature*. The two GBA games share the same world and
can interact via Game Boy Advance link cable with *Harvest Moon: A Wonderful Life* on GameCube.

You play as a young woman who purchases a run-down farm in Mineral Town. Your goal is to
restore the farm, grow crops, raise animals, make friends, and eventually get married.
The game runs on a seasonal calendar — Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter — each 30 days long.

## 3. Seasons & Calendar

Each season is exactly **30 days** long. The growing seasons are **Spring, Summer, and Fall**.
**Winter has no crops** — the ground is frozen and seeds cannot be planted outdoors.

### Season Transition Rules
- On **Day 1 of a new season**, all previous season's crops die immediately
- Dead crops must be removed with the **Sickle** before you can till and replant
- You **cannot plant on the last day of a season** (Day 30) for the following season —
  seeds planted out of season vanish overnight
- Grass is the **exception** — it survives season changes and regrows on its own

## 4. Festivals & Holidays

On festival days:
- **Shops are closed**
- **Zack does NOT pick up your shipment** — he picks it up the following day instead
- **You are generally busy and cannot harvest** (see timing details below)
- **Harvest Sprite hut remains open** from 9am–7pm — you can still hire sprites

### Spring

| Day | Festival | Time | Harvest Window |
|-----|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | New Year's Day Festival | 6pm – midnight | **Morning is free** |
| 14 | Spring Thanksgiving | Boys visit your farm every ~2hrs | Uncertain — they come to you |
| 18 | Spring Horse Race | 10am – 6pm | **Blocked** |
| 22 | Cooking Festival | 10am–noon (get dish), 4pm–6pm (judging) | Partial/risky |

> **Spring Thanksgiving:** High-affection bachelors visit your farmhouse throughout the day
> to bring you cookies. You may be able to harvest between visits but this is unconfirmed.
>
> **Spring Horse Race:** Bet on horses for medals. Medals can be exchanged for prizes.
> If your horse is fully grown, you can race it yourself. Winning the race for the first time
> awards a Power Berry. Prizes that can be sold: Adamantine (4 medals, 50G),
> Truffle (10 medals, 500G), Diamond (14 medals, 100G), Broach (18 medals, 2000G),
> Necklace (20 medals, 2000G). A full basket of Broaches = 540 medals = ~60,000G.

### Summer

| Day | Festival | Time | Harvest Window |
|-----|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Beach Opening Day | 10am – 6pm | **Blocked** (seeds also unavailable) |
| 7 | Chicken Festival | 10am – 6pm | **Blocked** |
| 20 | Cow Festival | 10am – 6pm | **Blocked** |
| 24 | Fireworks Festival | 6pm – midnight | **Morning is free** |

> **Beach Opening Day (Summer 1):** Seeds cannot be purchased from the Supermarket or Won
> on this day. Buy seeds on Summer 30 of the previous year to prepare.
>
> **Chicken Festival:** Chickens fight/sumo each other. You can win even with a 0-heart
> chicken. Winning grants the chicken the ability to eventually produce Gold/P/X eggs.
>
> **Cow Festival:** Judging contest based on your cow's affection level. Winning grants
> the cow the ability to eventually produce Gold/P/X milk.

### Fall

| Day | Festival | Time | Harvest Window |
|-----|----------|------|----------------|
| 3 | Music Festival | 6pm – midnight | **Morning is free** |
| 5 | Ann's Mom's Memorial | Unknown | Unknown — possibly a minor scene |
| 9 | Harvest Festival | 10am – 6pm | **Blocked** |
| 13 | Festival at Mother's Hill | 6pm – midnight | **Morning is free** |
| 18 | Fall Horse Race | 10am – 6pm | **Blocked** |
| 21 | Sheep Festival | 10am – 6pm | **Blocked** |
| 30 | Pumpkin Festival | Children visit your farm | **Likely free** |

> **Harvest Festival:** Bring food to add to a communal stew at Rose Square.
> Bringing poisonous mushroom or red grass causes everyone to get sick.
>
> **Fall Horse Race:** Same mechanics as Spring Horse Race. Same prizes. Awards a Power Berry
> for winning if not already earned in Spring.
>
> **Sheep Festival:** Your sheep must have its wool intact the day before the event —
> do not shear your entry sheep in the week leading up to Fall 21.
>
> **Pumpkin Festival:** Buy 3 chocolates beforehand. Stu, May, and Popuri will visit
> your farm every 2 hours during the morning.

### Winter

| Day | Festival | Time | Harvest Window |
|-----|----------|------|----------------|
| 2 | Thomas' Winter Request | Unknown | Unknown |

| 24 | Starry Festival | 6pm – 9pm | N/A (no crops) |
| 25 | Stocking Festival | Sleep between 9pm–midnight | N/A (no crops) |
| 30 | New Year's Eve | 6pm – midnight (Rose Square + Mother's Hill) | N/A (no crops) |

> **Starry Festival:** In the girl version, the bachelor with the highest affection for you

>

## 5. The Farm

### Farm Dimensions
- **43 tiles wide × 25 tiles tall** (1,075 total tiles)
- Confirmed by in-game measurement

### Farm Layout
```
[ House | Mailbox ]  [ Doghouse | Barn | Woodbin | Stable ]
[ Shipment Box ]
[ Tree (honey) ]   [                              ]
[ Fish Pond    ]   [        Your Crop Field        ]
[ Mill | Coop  ]   [                              ]
```

### Key Farm Locations

| Location | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Farmhouse | Sleep, cook, watch TV, save game |
| Mailbox | Receive letters, announcements, seeds ordered |
| Shipment Box | Drop items here by 5pm for Zack to collect |
| Fish Pond | Store caught fish (up to 100 of each size) |
| Tree | Gather Honey daily; Apple tree drops apples every Fall |
| Mill | Put Corn in to make 30 bushels of Chicken Feed per ear |
| Chicken Coop | Houses chickens; collect eggs; place Mayonnaise Maker here |
| Barn | Houses cows and sheep; collect milk and wool; place Cheese/Yarn Maker here |
| Woodbin | Stores chopped lumber; use for fences or fireplace (Winter) |
| Stable | Houses your horse |
| Doghouse | Houses your dog (it rarely actually sleeps there) |

### Fencing
- Lumber fences rot over time and must be replaced
- **Stone fences do not rot** and keep wild dogs out permanently
- **Branches also work as fences** and do not rot
- Only you, your horse, your dog, and Harvest Sprites can pass through stone fences
- Animals occasionally escape stone fences during mornings — check your farm map daily

| Silver | Silver Ore | 2,000G | Mine Level 1+ |

> **Money-saving tip:** Skip straight to Mystrile instead of upgrading step-by-step.
> Step-by-step costs up to 10,000G per tool. Mystrile costs only 4,000G.
> You can train any tool to Mystrile-ready by using it repeatedly near the Hot Spring
> and recovering stamina between sessions.

### Watering Can Upgrade Importance
- **Silver Watering Can or better:** Can reach the center tile of a 3×3 Full Block formation
- Without it, use 8-tile or 7-tile formations, or rely on a Harvest Sprite to water the center

### Cursed, Blessed, and Mythic Tools
Found in the Winter/Lake Mine. Cursed tools cannot be unequipped until Carter lifts the curse.
Once blessed they are more powerful and freely equippable. Mythic tools are the most powerful
in the game and require all 6 Blessed tools plus Mythic Stones.

| Tool | Found (Winter Mine Floor) | How to Bless |

| Cursed Fishing Rod | Level 29 | Use 225 times |
| Cursed Hoe | Level 39 | Have Carter bless it 10 times |
| Cursed Axe | Level 49 | Use 225 times |
| Cursed Hammer | Level 59 | Equip 10 consecutive days |
| Cursed Watering Can | Level 69 | Have Carter bless it 10 times |

## 7. Crops

### How Crops Work

Each crop progresses through **named growth stages**. Watering advances the crop one day
through its current stage. Missing a watering pauses growth — no setback, no damage.

```
Non-regrowable:   Seeds → [stages] → Ripe → (you replant from seeds)
Regrowable:       Seeds → [stages] → Ripe/Flowers → (snaps back to mid-stage) → Ripe → ...
```

**Regrow time** = sum of days in the stages from the return point back to Ripe/Flowers.

Crops die when the season ends — even regrowables mid-cycle.

## 8. Farm Grass

- **Stages:** Seeds → X → Y → Ripe
- **Stage days:** 3, 4, 4
- **First growth:** 11 days
- **Regrow:** Automatic after cutting — exact return stage unconfirmed (~4 days per Gwendy/Sharan, ~"once a week" per Franck)
- **Does NOT need watering** — plant in full 3×3 blocks freely
- **Cut with Sickle** → automatically transfers to grain silo as fodder
- **Seed cost:** 500G | **Buy:** Supermarket | **Available:** Spring, Summer, Fall only
- Grass **survives season transitions** and regrows on its own each spring

### Grass Uses
- Cut and stored in silo → placed in animal feeding bins
- Animals left outside eat 1 tile of mature grass per day
- You need roughly 7 tiles of grass per animal per week outdoors (so grass has time to regrow)
- For 16 barn animals maximum: ~112 grass tiles (≈12.5 seed bags worth)

## 9. Harvest Sprites

Seven small sprites live in a hut behind the Church. Befriending them allows you to hire
them to work on your farm for several days at a time.

### The Seven Sprites

| Name | Color | Birthday |
|------|-------|----------|
| Bold | Purple | Spring 4 |
| Staid | Blue | Spring 15 |
| Aqua | Teal / Indigo | Spring 26 |
| Timid | Green | Summer 16 |
| Hoggy | Yellow | Fall 10 |
| Chef | Red | Fall 14 |
| Nappy | Orange | Winter 22 |

### Building Friendship

- **Minimum 3 hearts** required before a sprite will work for you
- **Best gift:** Flour (50G, Supermarket) — cheap and highly effective
- **Gift-wrapping** adds a significant bonus to affection gain
- Sprites also love herbs matching their color (found foraging)
- **Birthday gifts** give the largest single-day affection boost
- Hut hours: **9am–6pm** normally; **9am–7pm on festival days**

### Hiring Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Contract lengths | 1, 3, or 7 days |
| Work start | The **next day** after hiring |
| Day off | Sprites need **1 day off** after contract ends before rehiring |
| Early dismissal | Talk to sprite on your field → choose "You're Done" |
| Rehire timing | Check Earnings screen — when you see "1D" left, hire replacement that day |

### Skill Categories

Each sprite has three independent skill tracks. Skill improves by working or playing
minigames at their hut. Higher skill = faster movement and task completion.

| Task | Coverage | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Watering** | 2 sprites covers a 12×24 field at low skill | Skills up quickly with regular hiring |
| **Harvesting** | 1 sprite usually sufficient | More may be needed if all crops ready same day |
| **Animal Husbandry** | 1 sprite handles all animals | **Never uses Makers** — ships raw milk/wool, not cheese/yarn |

> **Important:** If you want cheese or yarn, do NOT assign a sprite to Animals.
> They will always ship the raw product instead of processing it.

### Managing Sprite Schedules

Because sprites need a day off between contracts, you need to **stagger your hires**:
- Have a second sprite ready to start the day after the first one finishes
- On rain days, fire your watering sprites and rehire them the next day
  (rain waters crops automatically, so watering sprites are wasted that day)
- On days with no harvests expected, fire harvest sprites and rehire for the next harvest

## 12. Fishing

Get a free **Fishing Rod** from Zack (visit his house 11am–4pm in Spring, Year 1 with an empty tool slot).

Fish sizes and base prices:
| Size | Price |
|------|-------|
| Small | 50G |
| Medium | 120G |
| Large | 200G |

Fish can be stored in your **Fish Pond** (up to 100 of each size). You do not need to refrigerate them.

Upgrade your rod at Saibara's to catch bigger and rarer fish. Rod levels:
Normal → Copper → Silver → Gold → Mystrile → Cursed/Blessed/Mythic

### King Fishes
Watch the Fishing Show on TV (Tuesdays) for clues on where to find King Fish.
Each King Fish caught earns 10 Farm Degree points. Catch all 6 for 60 points.

## 15. Making Money

### Crop Income (first year priorities)
- **Spring:** Mix of Turnips (fast cash) and Cabbages (high return) — Cabbage is most profitable
- **Summer:** Prioritize Pineapple; also grow Corn for Chicken Feed
- **Fall:** Focus on Sweet Potato — most profitable crop in the game by a large margin

### Harvest Goddess Game (Spring 1–5 only)
- Watch TV on the Left Channel in Spring Days 1–5
- Higher/Lower number guessing game — play up to 5 times per day
- Win 10–14 in a row → **Relaxation Tea Leaves** (1,000G each)
- Save before playing, reload if you don't get the leaves
- Potential: ~5,000G per day for the first 4–5 days of Spring

### Foraging
- Fall foraging is most profitable (~3,060G/day) especially with Truffle access
- Summer grasses are worth collecting while clearing fields

### Horse Race / Medals
- Use the save/reload trick to bet on high-payout horses (x7–x15 odds)
- Fill a basket with Broaches (18 medals each, sell for 2,000G) for ~60,000G per race day

### Animals (long-term income)
- **Cows are the best money-makers** among animals
- Maximize hearts → upgrade to Large milk → win festival → eventually P and X quality
- Build the Cheese Maker for even higher value (Cheese X = 1,500G vs Milk X = 800G)
- **Sprites will not use Makers** — you must process milk/wool yourself if you want cheese/yarn

### Mining
- Alexandrite and Pink Diamond (10,000G each) in Winter Mine are the best ore income
- Mythic Stone (20,000G) in Spring Mine — rare, requires all Blessed tools

### Power Berries (important for stamina/income capacity)
Each Power Berry permanently increases your stamina, letting you do more work each day.

| Berry | How to Get |
|-------|-----------|
| Regular (×1) | Till soil on your farm (random location) |
| Regular (×1) | Throw offerings in Goddess Pond for 10 consecutive days |
| Regular (×1) | Win Horse Race (first time) |
| Regular (×1) | Win Beach Day Frisbee contest |
| Regular (×1) | Spring Mine, Floor 100 |
| Regular (×1) | Winter Mine, Floor 19 |
| Regular (×1) | Behind the entrance to the Winter Mine |
| Regular (×1) | Fish from the sea in Winter with Mystrile Rod |
| Regular (×1) | Buy from Horse Race medals (900 medals) |
| Regular (×1) | TV Shopping Network (after buying everything else available) |
| **Blue Berry** (×1) | Throw a Cucumber in Mountain Lake for 10 consecutive days — Kappa gives it |

> The Blue Power Berry reduces the effect of bad weather on your stamina.
